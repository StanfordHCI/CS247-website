HCI design studio

CS 247 · Winter 2015

This class will deepen your fluency with design prototyping and critique for interactive technology. To do this, you'll learn techniques for rapidly brainstorming and prototyping multiple interface alternatives — and why rapid prototyping is essential to excellent interaction design. You'll learn to communicate your ideas visually. You'll deepen your experiences with needfinding and continuous feedback. And you will do all this in a design studio environment, learning to critique and be critiqued with industry leaders who hold years of experience.

Project 3, Design Sprint: Needfinding and brainstorming

basic project prompt: ambient or whole-body interaction in a room/public space. Exact domain is driven by your studio theme, but needs to have some element of public space or embodied interaction in a room. Assume you have a webcam pointing at a wall, we'll write a localhost Python background subtraction routine that you can access via Javascript (a la Myron Krueger). You get a projector pointed at the wall.

The assignment will basically have three components: 1) go do needfinding and synthesize what you see. 2) brainstorm, 3) implement a highly targeted prototype. Number 3 is critical, you only have a week, so you need to be very targeted about what you prototype. Take on too much and you'll be sunk. You can prototype a lot in a short period by focusing on the most important components to get across the idea
