Project 3: Design Sprint

P3 is a focused design sprint to deepen your expertise in the user-centered elements of the design thinking process. You will work in teams of 3 people (from your studio) to identify and solve a meaningful user need. The final deliverable, a rough prototype of your idea, will serve as your launching point for P4, the final project.

Goal

P1 focused on visual communication; P2, on interaction design. Now we rewind to the early-stage design process in order to overcome the newbie mistakes of 1) surface-level needfinding and 2) conservative designs.

Schedule and Feedback

As with P2, you can submit the P3 milestones at your own pace. There are more milestones with P3 than P2, so keep track of your progress and benchmark it against our recommendations. You will receive completion points as you pass each milestone, and √-, √ or √+ guidance from us. As before, submit a milestone via Canvas before any class — make sure to bring printouts to show in studio. You can re-do any milestones for a new assessment.

Below is our recommended timeline for Project 3. Remember that it may take several iterations of feedback on submissions before you and your team are ready to declare a milestone complete. So aim to submit your first version of each milestone at least one studio day before the deadline you want to hit.

  • Oct. 19 (week 5): P3.I — form team, identify area of interest, and perform needfiding observation
  • Oct. 26 (week 6): P3.II — needfinding interpretation, brainstorming
  • November 2 (week 7): P3.III — prototyping and user feedback
  • November 19 (week 9): P3.IV — working implementation
  • November 30 (week 10): P3.V — user study results and redesign plan
  • December 4 (week 10)  P3.VI — final presentation, hard deadline
  • December 7 (finals) @ 12:15pm: Final submission, hard deadline

Grading rubric

Each of the six milestones is worth five points. You will receive five points for completing the assignment satisfactorily, submitting it, and your whole team bringing your sketches to class for feedback.

The final presentation and final submission will be the only parts of the assignment where we assign additional points based on the quality of your work. They are worth 7 points and 23 points respectively, and more detailed rubrics are available on the final presentation and final submission pages.
