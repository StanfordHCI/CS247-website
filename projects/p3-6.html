Project 3, part VI: final presentation

Goal

Now it's time to show off your hard work! The final presentations will be on December 4th.

Pitch

You need to come up with a forty five-second pitch to the audience. You are welcome to design your pitch however any way that you feel will be compelling. We will present your slide(s) behind you as you present, and will automatically go on to the next group after forty five seconds. Keep in mind that your audience are likely design experts.

Look below for details on how to submit your presentation materials.

Poster

You will need a 20"x30" poster to tell judges about your project. This is a chance to show off your visual design skills. A common error is to put too much content on the poster. Keep it simple and convey the main concept. It should be geared towards pitching your project to viewers/judges and not about your design process. We will provide easels; you will need to print out and mount your poster on a stiff backing so that it can stand alone on the easel. Posters can be printed at Lathrop, Costco($25) or Walgreens ($24). They usually have a 1-2 day turnaround. You can order online and pick it up from the store.

Prototype

Bring it so you can show it off during the demo and poster session! The staff and judges will interact with it there. Prepare a user task or scenario (or maybe a small set of them) to run through on the prototype with visitors to your poster. Think in advance of how to demo your system with as little friction as possible. For example, can you have prepared accounts rather than needing everyone to make their own?

Submit

Pull everything together into your submission folder labeled "P3-6". This is due by Friday 10am so that we can compile the slide deck. Please include:

  • A slide with your project name
  • Either:
    • A 45-sec video about your project, or
    • A powerpoint(16:9 aspect ratio, pptx) presentation for your project. If your presentation is on multiple slides or uses animation, time the slides to advance automatically.
  • Your poster file, in PDF form.

Grading rubric

The grading in this section focuses on your presentation and poster materials. The grading for the overall prototype and demo will be attached to your final deliverable, due during finals.

Category Unsatisfactory Adequate Good Excellent
Presentation
7pt
 1: Presentation and poster are incomplete or insufficient. 3: Presentation and poster tend to obscure the main idea. 5: Presentation and poster are generally clear, but slightly muddy in their execution. 7: Presentation and poster communicate the design idea clearly and effectively.

If any of the deliverables are missing, we will reduce your score by 25% per deliverable.
